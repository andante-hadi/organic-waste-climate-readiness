# Pre-Submission Pitch Draft: Nature Sustainability

## Proposed Title

Reconciling the waste hierarchy with climate-readiness priorities for global organic municipal waste

## Editorial Summary

Organic municipal waste is a major source of avoidable methane, but countries lack a global decision framework for translating the waste hierarchy into climate investment priorities. We combine World Bank What a Waste 3.0, World Development Indicators, Ember electricity carbon intensity, ecoinvent-derived parameters, and global boundary data to compare prevention, composting, anaerobic digestion with electricity recovery, and anaerobic digestion with biomethane/bio-CNG across 217 countries and economies. The analysis shows that prevention remains the most robust country-level strategy, but near-term climate priorities shift where methane baselines, energy substitution and implementation readiness make biological treatment or bio-CNG strategically important. The contribution is a climate-readiness framework that reconciles waste hierarchy principles with country-specific mitigation potential and infrastructure constraints.

## Significance Statement

The waste hierarchy rightly prioritizes prevention, yet prevention alone does not answer where countries should direct climate finance for unavoidable or already-generated organic waste. This study shows that organic municipal waste transitions require a spatial decision logic that combines hierarchy preference, methane avoidance, energy substitution, uncertainty and system readiness. The framework identifies immediate priorities, strategic build-out cases, no-regret complementary contexts and longer-term local-fit opportunities, offering a policy-relevant bridge between global methane mitigation, circular economy and waste-system transformation.

## Three-Sentence Cover-Letter Core

Dear Editors,

We submit our manuscript, "Reconciling the waste hierarchy with climate-readiness priorities for global organic municipal waste," for consideration as an Article in Nature Sustainability. The manuscript develops a global country-level screening framework that combines waste generation and treatment data, energy-system carbon intensity, ecoinvent-derived process parameters, uncertainty analysis and implementation-readiness indicators to evaluate prevention, composting, AD-electricity and AD-bio-CNG pathways for food and green organic municipal waste. We believe the work fits Nature Sustainability because it provides a cross-disciplinary policy framework for operationalizing the waste hierarchy under real-world climate, circularity, infrastructure and development constraints.

## Key Results To Highlight

- Food and green organic municipal waste reached approximately 1.064 billion tonnes in 2022 across 217 countries and economies.
- Unmanaged or disposal-associated organic waste corresponds to a first-pass methane burden of approximately 514 Mt CO2e per year.
- AD-bio-CNG gives the largest deterministic global GWP100 benefit, but prevention is the most robust country-level winner under uncertainty.
- Readiness analysis separates 43 immediate-priority countries/economies from 27 strategic build-out cases, 64 no-regret/complementary contexts and 18 longer-term/local-fit contexts.
- The results support the waste hierarchy while showing that hierarchy alone is insufficient for country-level climate investment.

## One-Line Takeaway

The paper does not ask whether prevention, composting, AD or bio-CNG is universally best; it asks how countries should sequence these pathways when hierarchy, climate opportunity and readiness point in different directions.
