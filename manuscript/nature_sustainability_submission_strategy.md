# Nature Sustainability Analysis Direct-Submission Strategy

## Target Content Type

Submit as an **Analysis**. The study is a new comparative analysis of existing waste, development, electricity, LCA-parameter and spatial datasets, leading to a policy-relevant decision framework. This is a better fit than Article because the manuscript does not present a new experimental dataset; it presents a new synthesis and modelling framework.

The current route is direct submission rather than presubmission inquiry. This avoids delaying the manuscript while preserving the option to redirect quickly if the paper is desk rejected.

## Core Pitch

Organic municipal waste is usually governed through the waste hierarchy, but climate action requires knowing where prevention, methane avoidance, biological treatment, bioenergy recovery, and infrastructure readiness align or diverge. This paper provides a global country-level climate-readiness framework showing that prevention remains the most robust principle, while AD-electricity, AD-bio-CNG, and composting have spatially specific roles for unavoidable or already-generated organic waste.

## Why Nature Sustainability

The journal fit is strongest when the manuscript is framed as an integrated sustainability study rather than a waste-technology comparison. The work connects:

- climate mitigation and methane reduction;
- food-waste prevention and downstream waste consequences;
- circular economy and resource recovery;
- waste, energy, nutrient, soil, and urban infrastructure systems;
- Global North/Global South differences in capacity and readiness;
- policy prioritization under uncertainty.

## Main Novelty Claim

The waste hierarchy is necessary but insufficient for spatially prioritizing organic municipal waste climate action. A country-specific framework is needed to reconcile hierarchy preference, marginal climate opportunity, uncertainty, and implementation readiness.

## Main Text Structure

1. Introduction: food/organic waste as a climate, circularity, and readiness problem.
2. Results 1: global food and green organic municipal waste burden.
3. Results 2: deterministic pathway comparison across prevention, composting, AD-electricity, and AD-bio-CNG.
4. Results 3: uncertainty and robust country-level winners.
5. Results 4: readiness typology and policy sequencing.
6. Discussion: hierarchy is not wrong; it needs spatial operationalization.
7. Methods: data, assumptions, pathway equations, readiness index, uncertainty.

## Keep In Supplementary

- Full ecoinvent extraction notes and licensing caveats.
- Detailed pathway equations and parameter tables.
- Sensitivity parameter distributions.
- Missing-data tables and Natural Earth join diagnostics.
- Regional and income-group tables.
- Additional maps such as AD-minus-prevention.

## Language To Use

- "climate-readiness screening framework"
- "operationalizing the waste hierarchy"
- "marginal climate opportunity"
- "implementation readiness"
- "unavoidable or already-generated organic waste"
- "screening framework, not a national inventory"

## Language To Avoid

- "debunking the waste hierarchy"
- "AD beats prevention"
- "best technology globally"
- "final national emissions inventory"
- "ecoinvent proves"

## Editorial Risk And Response

Risk: The paper may look like a technical LCA comparison.
Response: Put hierarchy, readiness, uncertainty, and policy sequencing at the center.

Risk: The paper may overclaim from screening-level LCA parameters.
Response: Clearly disclose that process emissions, biogas yield and biogas net calorific value come from ecoinvent 3.12 Cutoff unit-process exports and documentation, and methane-equivalent share is derived from the reported net calorific value. Treat AD-bio-CNG as a screening opportunity, not a universal recommendation, and point to Monte Carlo and bio-CNG stress tests.

Risk: Composting may look undervalued.
Response: State that GWP100-only screening does not fully capture soil carbon, nutrient cycling, cost, simplicity, local acceptability, or low-infrastructure deployability.

Risk: Prevention uncertainty is large.
Response: Use this as a strength of the analysis: prevention remains the most robust country-level winner despite wide uncertainty in achievable prevention rates and upstream emissions.

## Backup Path

If Nature Sustainability rejects the paper because the contribution is too applied or technical, the manuscript can be refocused for Resources, Conservation & Recycling by foregrounding resource recovery, LCA-informed pathway comparison, and circular-economy implementation.

If Nature Sustainability rejects the paper specifically because the LCA parameter layer is still too screening-level, then the next step is to run full openLCA product-system calculations or add literature/meta-analysis validation before resubmitting elsewhere.

## Current-Data Submission Logic

The first Nature Sustainability attempt should use the current data and the new ecoinvent 3.12 openLCA exports. The manuscript should remain transparent, uncertainty-aware and conservative. If the LCA parameter layer becomes decisive during review, the next improvement is full product-system calculation rather than another proxy update.

## Immediate Submission Tasks

1. Finalize author list, affiliations and corresponding-author details.
2. Convert the manuscript and Supplementary Information into journal-ready Word or LaTeX files.
3. Generate final multi-panel figures and export separate high-resolution files.
4. Format references and supplementary references.
5. Prepare suggested reviewers, reviewer exclusions if needed, CRediT roles, competing-interest statement and AI-use disclosure.
6. Prepare a clean public repository or archived package excluding proprietary ecoinvent files.
