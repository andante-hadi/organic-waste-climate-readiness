# Schimel-Style Revision Note

This note records the writing-style changes made to the main article draft using principles associated with Joshua Schimel's *Writing Science*.

## Revision Principles Applied

### Story before information

The manuscript now opens with a problem rather than a topic. The central tension is:

> The waste hierarchy says prevention should come first, but countries still need to manage large already-generated organic waste flows that create methane now.

This gives the paper a narrative problem that the analysis resolves.

### Opening, challenge, action, resolution

The Introduction now follows a clearer arc:

1. Food waste begins as a food-system problem and becomes a methane problem.
2. The waste hierarchy gives direction but not a full investment rule.
3. Existing evidence leaves a country-level decision gap.
4. The study builds a framework to fill that gap.
5. The contribution is a spatial decision logic linking hierarchy, methane opportunity, uncertainty and readiness.

### Characters and actions

Sentences were revised so that key actors do things:

- "Food waste begins..."
- "Policy has..."
- "Countries need..."
- "The readiness screen separates..."
- "The framework explains..."

This reduces passive, report-like phrasing.

### Topic sentences as signposts

Most paragraphs now begin with a claim that frames the paragraph:

- "Countries generate enough food and green waste for downstream management to matter."
- "Uncertainty changes the interpretation..."
- "Mitigation potential does not equal deployability."
- "The results sharpen the waste hierarchy rather than reject it."

### Stress position

Important ideas were moved toward paragraph and sentence endings where possible:

- "a near-term climate, sanitation and circular-economy challenge"
- "a way to keep prevention first while still prioritizing unavoidable or already-generated waste"
- "a spatial decision logic that links hierarchy, methane opportunity, uncertainty and readiness"

### Less hedging, clearer claims

The revision keeps scientific caution but avoids making every sentence sound like a limitation. Limits now appear where they help define interpretation, rather than interrupting the story.

## What Still Needs Style Work

- The Methods section remains technical and should later be tightened for flow after final parameter decisions.
- Figure captions should be rewritten again after final multi-panel figures exist.
- Once ecoinvent 3.12 access is resolved, the limitation paragraph should be updated so it does not over-emphasize temporary access constraints.
